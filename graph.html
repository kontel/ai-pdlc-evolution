Signal Graph

Every signal logged on Signals, plotted as a node in a continuously growing graph. Time runs horizontally, the three layers (plus cross-cutting) form swimlanes, and edges connect items that share tags — so vendor clusters, thematic clusters, and recurring rails become visible at a glance. Switch views to reframe the same data along a different axis.

What the dimensions mean

The graph is fed directly from signals.html — no separate dataset to keep in sync. Every Sunday-evening refresh that lands a new week block automatically grows the graph the next time the page is opened.

  • X (time) — chronological week of publication. Newest weeks land on the right, older weeks accumulate to the left as the log grows.
  • Y (layer) — the three-layer future (ambient · agentic · ephemeral) plus the cross-cutting rail. In Timeline view this is a swimlane; in Constellation view it becomes node colour with force-driven clustering.
  • Edges — two signals are linked if they share at least two tags. Vendor mass (Anthropic, Google, Microsoft, OpenAI) and theme mass (Trust, Toolchain, Orchestration) emerge as visual clusters without any manual grouping.
  • Node size — degree (number of tag-shared neighbours). Hub signals — the ones that anchor a theme — render larger.
